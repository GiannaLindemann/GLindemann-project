The Creative Process

Making Sense of the World

Creating art is about exploring new ideas, images, and creations. Art practices can be used to make sense of the outside world including nature and community and global events. We can learn about nature through observational painting and drawing as well as creating with collected leaves and flowers. Drawing animals can be a great way to add a visual element to processing and retaining scientific information. Through art we can express human conditions and work through experiences such as love, loneliness, isolation, relationships, uncertainty, sadness, pride, togetherness, and joy. For non verbal people and those who struggle to express feelings through words, art can provide relief, solace and a space for processing.

Moving Through Trauma

For many, creating with our hands and expressing ideas on a paper can be easier than formulating written or spoken words. Art therapy is a proven method for healing trauma. While making art we are able to tap into our mind body connection and strengthen connection between our physical and mental experiences. It can also be used to develop self confidence and identity. Each individual has a unique approach to art making, and there are no wrong answers. Art can be messy and frustrating but that is part of its valuable complexity.

Self Expression & Acceptance

When people are first approaching a project they may be worried they don't have the technical skills or creative ideas required to execute a respectable result. The thing is---- they will never develop those skills and ideas if they don't practice! Art is all about diving in- brainstorming, trying a variety of approaches. As the late Bob Ross said, “There are no mistakes in art. Only happy accidents.” Unintentional marks, strokes and shapes happen in art all the time. You may come to the conclusion that a piece is not bringing you joy anymore or you want to restart in a different way, but I always encourage my students to look at accidental lines and see what they can become. And a bonus is that when you're painting you can just add another layer!
